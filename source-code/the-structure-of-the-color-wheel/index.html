The Color Wheel

The color wheel is organized and grouped into 3 categories:

  • Primary colors:
    • Primary colors in the RGB color wheel are the colors that, added together, create pure white light. These colors are: red, green and blue.
    • Primary colors in the RYB color wheel, primary colors are colors that canâ€™t be mixed from other colors. There are three primary colors: red, yellow, and blue.
  • Secondary colors are created when primary colors are mixed:
    • In the RGB color wheel there are three secondary colors:
      1. Cyan (when you mix green and blue make cyan),
      2. Magenta (when you mix blue and red make magenta),
      3. Yellow (when you mix light, red and green make yellow).
    • In the RYB color wheel, the secondary colors are:
      1. Purple (red mixed with blue),
      2. Orange (red mixed with yellow),
      3. Green (yellow mixed with blue).
  • Tertiary colors are colors made by combining a secondary color with a primary color.
    • In the RGB color wheel there are six tertiary colors:
      Orange, Chartreuse green, Spring green, Azure, Violet, Rose.
    • In the RYB color wheel, the secondary colors are:
      Red-orange, Yellow-orange, Yellow-green, Blue-green, Blue-violet, Red-violet.
