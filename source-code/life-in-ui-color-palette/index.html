Before starting our work:

  • We should determine what exactly we are trying to deliver to the reader, the theme of the project.
  • We should understand that the eye focuses on a certain way and our mind reads the information on a certain pattern.

If we take in consideration that any object/subject of interest when hit by light has:

  • Light parts: where it is most lit up, those being called highlights.
  • Dark parts: where there is less light to no light, those being called shadows (tones).

The light grabs the user’s attention, so using the primary colors (those that stand out the most) is advisable. The highlights should stand out the most against your background. If the background is dark, the color that stands out the most against it is going to be bright and vice versa. By shining more light on certain elements, they stand out more to the user, by toning them down slightly, they fade more into the background.

The mid-tones are the slightly less relevant secondary elements. The mid-tones will grab less attention than the highlight. Anything with less contrast fades more into the background.

While the tone or shadow wrapping around everything is the background. This should make the highlight and mid tones stand out. This color should be the backdrop for the main “focus elements” to come to life against.
